Vector Spaces, Span & Determinants

A vector space is any set that's closed under addition and scalar multiplication. Below, play with two vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^2\) and watch how their span, linear independence, and determinant are really the same story told three ways.

What makes something a vector space?

A set \(V\) with addition and scalar multiplication is a vector space if, for all \(u, v, w \in V\) and scalars \(c, d\):

  • \( u + v \in V \) and \( cu \in V \) (closure)
  • \( u + v = v + u \) (commutative)
  • \( (u + v) + w = u + (v + w) \) (associative)
  • There exists \( \mathbf{0} \in V \) with \( u + \mathbf{0} = u \)
  • Every \( u \) has an inverse \( -u \) with \( u + (-u) = \mathbf{0} \)
  • \( c(u+v) = cu + cv \) and \( (c+d)u = cu + du \)

Subspace test

A subset \( H \subseteq V \) is a subspace if and only if:

  1. \( \mathbf{0} \in H \)
  2. \( H \) is closed under addition
  3. \( H \) is closed under scalar multiplication

Rank & Nullity

The Rank-Nullity Theorem says that for an \( m \times n \) matrix \( A \):

\[ \operatorname{rank}(A) + \operatorname{nullity}(A) = n \]

Worked example. Row reduce:

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 & 3 \\ 1 & 2 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \;\xrightarrow{R_2 - 2R_1,\; R_3 - R_1}\; \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \;\xrightarrow{R_3 - R_2}\; \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

Two nonzero rows remain, so \( \operatorname{rank}(A) = 2 \). With \( n = 3 \) columns, nullity \( = 3 - 2 = 1 \) — there's a one-dimensional null space, spanned by \( (-2, 1, 0) \) since column 2 is a free variable tied to column 1.
